A payer's price is a multiple of Medicare.

Every plan's negotiated rate as a multiple of the free public CMS schedule (Medicare OPPS for procedures & imaging, CLFS for labs) — one hospital, one service line, and one pricing method at a time. Hospitals price some codes as a set dollar (fee schedule) and others as a fraction of their list price (% of charges); we keep those lanes separate so nothing is blended or inflated. A taller bar = a more expensive deal.

One hospital, one service line, one pricing method at a time — a plan's multiplier varies by all three, so pooling would mislead. Bars are the median × CMS across that plan's codes, and each payer bar is divided into its distinct plans (many thin slices = a granular, per-plan-negotiated deal; one or two = a blanket rate). Methods: fee schedule = a set dollar per code; % of charges = a set fraction of the hospital's list price (shown as the resulting dollar ÷ CMS, and as the implied % of list in the strip above). Case rates (per admission) and per diems (per day) are bundled prices, not per-code — counted in the method mix but never shown as a per-code multiple. Tiny samples (<12 codes) dropped. Source: byte-exact AZ MRF captures × CMS OPPS Addendum B / CLFS 2025.

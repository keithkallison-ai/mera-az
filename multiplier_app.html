One hospital, one service line at a time — a plan's multiplier varies by line, so pooling would mislead. Bars are the median × CMS across that plan's codes, and each payer bar is divided into its distinct plans — many thin slices = a granular, per-plan-negotiated deal; one or two = a blanket rate. Employer/self-funded groups that share a contract are collapsed by multiplier; tiny samples (<12 codes) are dropped. Hospitals that price mostly as %-of-charges are flagged — there “× CMS” reflects inflated gross, not a negotiated multiple. Source: byte-exact AZ MRF captures × CMS OPPS Addendum B / CLFS 2025.
